Hue
The noun hue means both a color and a shade of a color. Green is a hue, and turquoise is a hue of both green and blue.
Saturation
Saturation refers to the intensity or purity of a color. A highly saturated color appears vivid and rich, while a desaturated color looks more muted and gray.
Brightness
Brightness is the perception of how light or dark a color appears. It is influenced by the amount of light that reaches the eye and can be adjusted in design software.
Opacity
Opacity refers to the transparency of a color. An opaque color is solid and completely blocks the view of anything behind it, while a transparent color allows light to pass through and reveals the background.
Contrast
Contrast is the difference in luminance or color that makes an object distinguishable. High contrast between elements can help to draw attention and improve readability.
Lightness
Lightness is the perception of how light or dark a color appears, regardless of its actual brightness. It is often used in color models to describe the relative lightness of different colors.
Alpha Transparency
Alpha transparency refers to the level of transparency in a color, with 0 being completely transparent and 1 being completely opaque. It is commonly used in digital graphics to create layered effects.
