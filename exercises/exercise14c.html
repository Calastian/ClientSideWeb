Types of bicycles

Road Bike

Road bikes are lightweight, high-performance bicycles built for speed and efficiency on paved surfaces like roads and cycle paths. Key features include drop-bar handlebars, skinny 700c wheels with smooth tires, a lightweight frame, and a wide range of gears for handling varied terrain. They are designed for riders who want to cover long distances quickly and offer a fast, comfortable, and technologically sophisticated riding experience

Mountain Bike

Mountain bikes, or MTBs, are specialized bicycles designed for off-road riding on rough terrain, featuring durable frames, knobby tires, and suspension systems for shock absorption. Key components include powerful brakes (often hydraulic disc brakes), wide handlebars for control, and wide-ratio gearing for steep climbs and descents. Different types of mountain bikes exist, such as cross-country, trail, and downhill, each suited for specific terrains and riding styles based on factors like suspension travel.

Gravel Bike

Gravel bikes are versatile bicycles designed to handle both paved and unpaved surfaces, blending features of road and mountain bikes to offer a comfortable, efficient, and capable ride for adventure riding and commuting on mixed terrain. Key features include wide tire clearance for traction and comfort, a stable and relaxed frame geometry, disc brakes for superior stopping power, and often a variety of drive-train options for versatility.
